Subject line: Do not set alarm clocks!

Almost everyone will tell you that setting alarm clocks and having a schedule will solve your procrastination problems. While they are not totally wrong, you know how much you snooze your alarm clocks and shift your tasks down the calendar.

It’s okay, I do it too. In fact, almost everyone does it. However, it just may not be the long-term solution for someone who procrastinates.

Take Binta for example. She was my college friend, and she never studied or did her assignments in time, but always spent most of her time gaming. When Binta noticed exam dates drew near, she set her alarm clock everyday to read and put studying in her list of tasks.

On the first day, Binta stuck to time, dropped her game console when the alarm rung for studying, and read. The same happened on the second day and on the third.

After four days, Binta started to snooze her alarm clocks to increase her gaming tome. She started to have little time to study, and soon, she went back to not studying at all.

Binta’s story passes one message; Alarm clocks are not the solution to procrastination. They work for the moment but they do not solve it long term. You may now ask, ‘what then is the solution?’

I’ll tell you, but first, Have you ever done any activity without procrastinating? Have you ever asked yourself why you do not shift it to later and do it immediately?

In Binta’s case, she never procrastinates gaming, why? It’s probably because she loves it and it arouses no form of anxiety for her.
