Subject line: The #1 step no Freelancer should miss

When I talk to a freelancer and ask them why they are freelancing, pretty much most of them tell me it’s for the money.

I mean, who doesn’t like multiple streams of income, especially when it comes in the comfort of your home?

There was a time I used to write without getting paid. I wrote out of goodwill, people begged me to write for them and I did. Just like that. Yes, now I think it was pretty dumb.

But that was because I didn’t even know why I was freelancing and what I wanted from freelancing, which is the #1 of seven starter freelancing steps I want to tell you about.

What’s the end goal of your freelance career? Are you doing it to supplement your income? Do you want to be a limited freelancer (part-time freelancer)?

Just something you do on the weekends when pay is not good? Do you plan on expanding your career or you’re just looking for an escape from corporate jobs?

Whatever it is, the first step is knowing. If you don’t agree with me, then surely you must agree with Arthur D. Hlavaty who said, ‘The first secret of getting what you want is knowing what you want’.

I’m pretty certain most freelancers do not even sit to think of this. Most people just want to freelance because that’s what people are doing nowadays.

But only when you know why you’re freelancing can you bring your reality to match your dream. Oh wow, how that sounded nice.

So, before you go on your next freelance project, have a little conversation with yourself, you could write it down as well.

‘Why am I freelancing?’ ‘What’s the end goal of my freelance career?’ ‘What am I doing to match my reality to my goal?’
