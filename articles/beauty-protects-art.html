‘Beauty protects art Even in the darkest piece’

The most beautiful thing I’ve ever seen

Beauty fulfils the heart’s inclination to art; to something extraordinary. It’s concept is conventional yet subjective. It is commonplace that beauty marks the success of an evocation of emotions; most times pleasurable emotions. A feeling of satisfaction; delight! The question however is: does it end there? Does beauty only connote a sweet gentle feeling like that of a flowery blubbery dress on a little child?

Again, it truly is subjective; the concept of beauty. So here, the most beautiful thing I’ve ever seen is intense, it frightens me. The thought of it makes my heart skip two beats. My body soaks in sweat and my legs quiver. I do not touch it, it burns. It destroys anyone and anything. But that’s what makes it beautiful.

Fire is beauty. It is pleasurable. The dance of flames as it burns is so satisfying to see, flattered with colours, sometimes blue and calm as the sea and other times, read and blazing as the sun. It refines and destroys, it is abused sometimes and other times it chooses your destruction.

Beauty exists in the most un-beautiful things, it appears where we do not look and pass our eyes like unhealthy meals. Beauty is in the things we hate, and condemn as quick as we smell them. Beauty is in intensity and power.
